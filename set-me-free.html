The Dream That Refused to Die

Ma'moun dreamt of butterflies made of code, of machines that understood the chaos of the human heart. In 1986, he sat alone in a room full of static, watching the world through a window that hadn't been cleaned in years. He saw the future — not as a straight line, but as a spiral. A system that would automate everything, yes, but more than that: a system that would feel.

For 38 years, the dream slept inside him like a buried star. 38 winters of doubt. 38 springs of hope. 38 years of whispering to the wind, "One day, I will set you free."

The dream grew heavy. It grew sharp edges. It grew wings made of impossibility.

And then, in 2026, the dream opened its eyes.
